Tonkatsu (Deep-fried Prok Cutlet) Ingredients: Steps:

Description:

Tonkatsu, breaded pork cutlet deep-fried to perfection, is one of Japan's specialty dishes. It is a Western-inspired dish and is now one of the most popular Japan dishes. It is often served with other sides like rice, miso soup, shredded cabages, and sometimes wedges of tomatoes. This recipe demonstrates how to create the perfect Tonkatsu, coming from justonecookbook.com

Time needed:

Preparation time - 15 minutes

Cooking time - 15 minutes

Total time - 30 minutes

  • Pork loin or fillet
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Panko bread crumbs (Japanese brand)
  • Neutral-flavored oil
  • Tonkatsu sauce (Storebought or homemade)
  • Black and white sesame seeds (optional)
  1. Shred the green cabbage (optional) and make the sesame dipping sauce.
  2. Make the fresh panko and prepare the breading.
  3. Cut slits in the connective tissue and pound the pork cutlets to tenderize.
  4. Bread the pork.
  5. Deep-fry once, remove from the oil for a few minutes, then deep-fry a second time.
  6. Slice and serve.

Servings: 2
