Xiaolongbao (Soup Dumplings) Ingredients: Steps:

Description:

Xiao Long Bao, literally meaning “small basket buns”, is a type of steamed bun originating from Jiangsu province, China. Known as soup dumplings in English, they are a hybrid of Baozi (包子) and Jiaozi (饺子): having the shape of Baozi but using non-leavened wrappers like Jiaozi. This recipe demonstrates how to create delicious and hot Xialongbao, coming from redhousespice.com

Time needed:

Preparation time - 1 hour

Cooking time - 1 hour 10 minutes

Total time - 2 hours and 10 minutes

Aspic

  • 1 mediums-sized Whole Chicken (free-range or organic)
  • 3 stalks scallions - cut into halves
  • 6 slices ginger

Filling

  • 450 g Minced pork
  • 3 stalks scallions (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon Minced ginger
  • 1 tablespoon Light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing rice wine
  • 1/4 Ground white pepper
  • 1/4 Salt
  • 1/2 Sugar

Dipping Sauce

  • Red rice vinegar or Black rice vinegar
  • Ginger (julienned)

Wrappers

  • 180 g All-purpose Flour
  • 60 g Hot Water
  • 35 g Tap Water

Aspic

  1. Preheat the oven at 390°F/200°C/Gas 6 (Fan-assisted 360°F/180°C)
  2. Place chicken in an oven-proof pot/pan/baking tray. Put scallions and ginger inside the chicken. Cover TIGHTLY with a lid/tin foil.
  3. Cook in the oven for 1 hour. Then leave to cool (covered).
  4. Keep the chicken for other dishes. Pour the liquid into a container. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours until it turns into a piece of jelly-like aspic (about 250g).

Dough and Wrappers

  1. Firstly mix 180g flour with 60g hot water.
  2. Then add 35g tap water.
  3. Combine to form a rough dough then rest (covered) for 10 mins.
  4. Knead again until very smooth.
  5. Cover and rest for a further 30-60 minutes until it becomes soft.
  6. Roll the dough into a rope. Divide into 20 pieces. Then press into discs with the palm of your hand. Cover at all times when not using (with cling film/in a plastic bag/with an upside-down bowl, etc.).
  7. Always remember to dust the surface and your rolling pin with a little flour to prevent sticking as thin wrappers tear easily. If available, you could use cornstarch to dust. It has a finer texture and works even better.
  8. Use the roll & turn technique to flatten the wrappers (please refer to the tutorial video in the recipe card below). This way the wrappers are thicker in the middle and thinner on the edge.

Filling

  1. Mix all ingredients mentioned (pork and seasonings).
  2. Before adding in the aspic, remove the kayer of chicken fat with a spoon.
  3. Afterwards, use a fork to break it into tin pieces.
  4. Add the aspic to the pork filling and mix well.
  5. Remember to keep the prepared filling in the fridge and don’t bring it out until the moment you are ready to assemble.

Dumpling

  1. Always assemble the dumpling one by one as you roll out each wrapper.
  2. Add 35g of filling in the middle of the wrapper.
  3. Pleat into a round dumpling with about 15-20 folds on the wrapper.
  4. You can leave the center either sealed or leaved open.
  5. Remember to keep the prepared filling in the fridge and don’t bring it out until the moment you are ready to assemble.

    6. 6) Prepare the steamer basket to avoid sticking. Line the steamer basket with steamer parchment paper, or cut regular parchment paper into squares.

    7) Place dumplings in (leave at least 3cm/1inch space between them).

    7) Bring water to a full boil in a wok/pot. Then put the steamer basket on top. Turn the heat to medium-low. Steam for 8 mins (if cooking over 10 dumplings at a time, increase to 10 mins).


Servings: 20
